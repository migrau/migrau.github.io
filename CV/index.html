Personal statement

I am a bionformatic researcher working at the Disease Ecology and Evolutionary Genetics group, in the Department of Microbiology at Monash University. My main task consist in the analysis of Next Generation Sequencing data, especially focused in the evolution aspects of pathogens.

I design pipelines to solve a huge variety of questions. These tasks include the use of illumina/ionTorrent/pacbio/nanopore data to perform new assemblies, within host population analysis (iSNV), phylogenetics, virus-host interactions studies, analysis of factors affecting transmission of pathogens (bottlenecks), identification of positive selection, among others.

The pipelines make use of multiple software tools, as well as our own libraries and scripts, using mainly (but not only) Python and R.
